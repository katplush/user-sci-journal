Usability Testing

Excited to share my thoughts and findings

Posted by Katerina Plushko on April 21, 2019

This post is a report of the Usability test done on the website, Nomad List. Nomad List self describes itself as a database of 2300 plus cities in the world with thousands of data points analyzed to help travelers and more specifically digital nomads to analyze places the best fit their needs.

The ideal user base for this site are tech savvy travelers familiar with analyzing data sets. The testers used for this usability study ranged from high to moderate web users.

The Testers:

  1. JC - 34 year old Male, Software Engineer
    • Avg hours spent online daily: 8 hrs
    • Sites browsed: News pages, technical documentation blogs, Wikipedia, forum sites
    • Favorite sites: Stack overflow, Wikipedia

  2. OP - 30 year old Female, Insurance Account Specialist
    • Avg hours spent online daily: 12 hrs
    • Sites browsed: Social Media, travel sites, flight ticket sites, google images, Wikipedia, siccode.com
    • Favorite sites: Facebook

  3. VP - 57 year old Male, Self Employed Entrepreneur
    • Avg hours spent online daily: 2-3 1/2 hrs
    • Avg hours spent online daily: 2-3 1/2 hrs
    • Favorite sites: YouTube, Google Scholar search

The Script

The testing for this usability test was performed one on one with each of the testers mentioned above. All the testers were unfamiliar to the site before the usability testing began. I followed a similar format to the User Testing Script provided by Steve Krug. Each Tester was introduced to the usability test process and asked to detail their thoughts as the perused the site and completed the requested tasks.

Upon opening the link to the homepage, the testers were asked to review the homepage and give a brief description of what their thoughts were on what the purpose of the website was and what service it was providing.

Question 1: Look at this page and tell me what you make of it: what strikes you about it, whose site you think it is, what you can do here, and what itâ€™s for. Just look around and do a little narrative.

Then they were advised that I would be asking them to perform a set of 3 tasks. The 3 tasks requested of the testers are as follows:

Task 1: Please find the least expensive travel destination with the highest ranking

Task 2: Can you locate the map feature and do your best to explain the scale and scoring the website uses to score featured places on the map; Are you able to find a safe and warm destination using the map feature

Task 3: Can you figure out what it costs to join and would you feel comfortable purchasing one of the packages.

At the end they were asked to summarize their thoughts and experience on the page. To provide any additional thoughts on what they thought might be missing or confusing and what they liked.
